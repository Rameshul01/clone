Hi, I'm Ramesh sati

National Geographic contributor and wildlife photographer with 12 years shooting in India's most iconic national parks — from Ranthambore to Kaziranga, from the Himalayas to the Sundarbans mangroves.

What started as a childhood fascination with birds in my backyard in Delhi grew into a career that has taken me from Bengal tiger reserves to African savannas. I've had my photos published in NatGeo, BBC Wildlife, and Sanctuary Asia.

I started WildLens because I believe that great wildlife photography is a skill anyone can learn — and I want to share everything I know with you.

My Story

The Journey

2013
First Camera, First Forest

Bought my first DSLR with college savings. Drove to Jim Corbett alone, came back with 800 blurry photos — and a complete obsession with wildlife photography.

2015
First Publication — Cover of Wildlife of India

My photo of a Bengal Tiger at dawn was selected for the cover of Wildlife of India magazine. That moment confirmed this was my path.

2017
NatGeo Contributor & Africa Safari

Became a contributing photographer for National Geographic India. Led my first photography safari across Masai Mara capturing the Great Migration.

2019
Launched WildLens

Started WildLens as a YouTube channel. Within 6 months, 10,000 subscribers. Built proper structured courses based on student demand.

2025
2,400+ Students, 30+ Countries

WildLens now has students across India, UK, Australia, USA and beyond. One of India's top wildlife photography education platforms.
